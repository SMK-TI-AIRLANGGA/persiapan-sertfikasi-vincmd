Peminjaman buku

“Reading is not a race to finish books or a way to impress others. It is a quiet discipline that reshapes how you think, how you judge, and how you understand the world. Every page you read sharpens your mind, challenges your assumptions, and protects you from borrowed opinions. In a world that constantly demands your attention but rarely earns your trust, reading is an act of independence—because the person who reads learns to think, and the person who doesn’t slowly hands that power away.”
